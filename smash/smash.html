Welcome

Welcome to the web page for the PGHS SSBC (Pleasant Grove High School Super Smash Brothers Club)! Here you will find announcements and important information regarding the club.

Club Activities

All club activities will be in Ms. Yocom's classroom unless otherwise stated (Room 220, across the hall from the writing lab).

Standard Meetings

The SSBC will have weekly meetings every Tuesday during lunch, with the exception of the second Tuesday of every month.

During these meeting we will primarily be playing Super Smash Brothers for Wii U and 3ds. Feel free to bring controllers and a 3ds. Please note that Wii U's and other expensive equipment may not be brought to these meetings. Some weeks we may also play Brawl (Wii), Melee (Gamecube), and the original (N64). We may also have lessons going over advanced topics many players may not know about.

Monthly Meetings

Every second week of the month we will meet on Friday after school.

These meetings will be larger than the standard meetings. More Wii U's and equipment will be brought, and mini competitions and special events will take place.

Tournaments

The SSBC will hold tournaments independant of the school tournament. Check the "Events" page for more info.

Joining the Club

In order to participate in club activities you must have turned in a club admission form signed by your parents for school requirements and the protection of equipment. You can pick up and turn in the club admission form from Ms. Yocom (Room 220). The form is also available for download on the "Info" page.

Recommended Tutorials

Hide/Show

These are tutorials that are some of the most important from the above resources. Some are fundementals, some are more complex techniques. They will all help you improve.

Directional Influence (DI)

A tutorial video on Directional Influence (DI) by My Smash Corner. There are three types of DI: Directional Influence, Tap/Smash DI, and Momentum Canceling. Directional Influence is when you aim for the direction you want to go when being launched. Tap DI or Smash DI is controlling the direction a character moves in multiple hit moves or combos. This can also be used to escape grabs at high percentages.

Ledge Punishes

A tutorial video on ledge punishes by My Smash Corner. Ledge punishing is a technique to prevent opponents from getting back on stage and taking a stock.

Perfect Pivoting

A tutorial video on perfect pivoting by My Smash Corner. A Perfect Pivot is a technique in Super Smash Bros. for Nintendo 3DS and Wii U that mimics Wavedashing. It is achieved by dashing in one direction, then quickly flicking the control stick in the other direction. This will cause the character to slide along the ground in the flicked direction.

Jab Locking

A tutorial video on Jab Locking by My Smash Corner. A lock is caused due to certain moves causing a character to bounce when they are on the ground after a missed tech, preventing them from using one of their wake up options long enough for the other player to hit them again with another move that can cause a lock.

B-Reversing and Wavebouncing

A tutorial video on B-Reversing and Wavebouncing by My Smash Corner. B-reversing refers to the act of performing a special move and changing the direction the character is facing at the same time while also reversing momentum.
